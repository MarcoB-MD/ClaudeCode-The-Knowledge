## Key Ideas

- **REBUS = Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics** — the model's core claim is that psychedelics relax the precision weighting of high-level priors (beliefs), which ordinarily constrain the rest of the brain's hierarchy from the top down. Precision here is inverse variance, equivalent to felt confidence.
- **The anarchic brain** — the complementary component: when top-down priors are relaxed, bottom-up information (especially from intrinsic limbic sources) travels more freely up the hierarchy. These two elements — REBUS and the anarchic brain — are mutually dependent and together define the model.
- **Integrating two frameworks** — REBUS synthesises the free-energy principle (Friston) and the entropic brain hypothesis (Carhart-Harris). The brain minimises free energy (surprise); psychedelics increase the entropy of spontaneous brain activity, enriching conscious experience by flattening the brain's energy landscape.
- **5-HT2A receptor as the trigger point** — psychedelics act primarily via 5-HT2ARs on deep-layer pyramidal neurons, which encode expectations and priors. These receptors are densest in high-level cortex, especially within the default-mode network (DMN). Their activation renders high-level priors more sensitive to ascending prediction errors, reducing their precision weighting.
- **Alpha oscillations and the DMN** — psychedelics reliably suppress alpha (and beta) oscillatory power, particularly in DMN regions. Alpha is associated with top-down inhibition and heavily weighted priors. The degree of alpha suppression in the posterior cingulate cortex correlates with the intensity of ego dissolution under both psilocybin and LSD.
- **Simulated annealing as a metaphor for therapy** — psychedelics create an "entropic hot state" of elevated neural plasticity, analogous to heating a metal so that it can be reshaped. Pathological priors (e.g., entrenched negative self-beliefs in depression) are relaxed in this state and can be revised via incoming bottom-up signals. As the drug is metabolised, the system "cools" and may settle into a new, revised landscape.
- **Dose dependency** — at low doses, effects are felt most at perceptual/visual levels (high 5-HT2AR expression in visual cortex). At high doses, the highest hierarchical levels (DMN, self/ego models) are substantially disrupted, producing ego dissolution and enabling lasting revision of high-level priors.
- **Psychedelics vs. psychosis** — both involve weakened high-level priors, but differ crucially: in early psychosis, ascending prediction errors are ascribed aberrant salience (via dopaminergic mechanisms), leading to crystallised delusional beliefs to stabilise the system. In the psychedelic state, uncertainty is contextually expected and typically integrated rather than defended against; delusional belief formation is rare.
- **Insight and Bayesian model reduction (BMR)** — psychedelics facilitate insight by enabling heavily weighted priors to be revised via BMR, a mechanism in which overfitted models are pruned. Acute self-reported insight during psilocybin-assisted therapy predicts long-term clinical improvement and positive personality change.
- **Criticality** — psychedelics tune the brain toward a critical point between order and disorder, making it maximally sensitive to perturbation (critical slowing). Like a liquid surface vs. a frozen lake — small inputs create large, enduring ripples. This state of heightened plasticity is the mechanistic window for belief revision.
- **Broader implications** — beyond mental illness, the model predicts that psychedelics can relax any overconfident high-level prior: rigid political, religious, or philosophical beliefs may also be subject to revision. The anarchic brain, by definition, is not selective about which priors it destabilises.
